[befőtt](https://en.bab.la/dictionary/hungarian-english/bef%C5%91tt)

> Elvan mint a befőtt

This Hungarian idiom is a classic! While a literal translation is **"living like a jar of preserves,"** that doesn’t quite capture the vibe in English.

In Hungarian, it usually implies someone is living a **quiet, passive, or sheltered life**—often because they have no other choice or they've simply settled into a dull routine.

Depending on the specific "flavor" of the situation, here are the best English equivalents:

### 1. The "Boring & Passive" Vibe

If you mean someone is just existing without any excitement or initiative:

* **"Vegetating"** – This is the closest in terms of energy. It means living a dull, inactive life.
* **"In a rut"** – If they are stuck in a repetitive, uninspired routine.
* **"Like a bump on a log"** – Usually refers to someone sitting around doing absolutely nothing.

### 2. The "Safe & Sheltered" Vibe

If the person is "preserved" from the harsh realities of the world:

* **"Living in a bubble"** – They are isolated from the real world.
* **"Kept in cotton wool"** – (British English) To be overprotected or overly sheltered.

### 3. The "Waiting" Vibe

Sometimes "elvan mint a befőtt" implies just sitting on a shelf waiting for something to happen:

* **"Gathering dust"** – If a person (or object) is being ignored or left unused for a long time.
