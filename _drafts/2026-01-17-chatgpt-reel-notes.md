### Line-by-line translation

**13 kiló vs. 1,5 tonna:**
*13 kilos vs. 1.5 tons:*

**muntják szarvas kezdett ki rinocérosszal**
a *muntjac deer* *picked a fight with* a *rhinoceros*

**egy lengyel állatkertben**
in a *Polish zoo*

### Notes on meaning & structure

* This is a **headline-style caption**, very compact and journalistic.
* The humour/appeal comes from the **absurd size contrast** (13 kg vs. 1.5 tons).
* **vs.** is read aloud as *versus*, just like in English.

### Vocabulary & phrases

* **kiló** – kilo, kilogram (colloquial)
* **tonna** – ton
* **muntják szarvas** – muntjac deer (a very small deer species)
* **rinocérosz** – rhinoceros
* **kezdett ki (vkivel)** – to pick on / to start a fight with / to provoke
* **állatkert** – zoo
* **lengyel** – Polish

### Usage note

* **kezdeni vkivel** is very common in spoken Hungarian:

  * *Ne kezdj ki velem!* – “Don’t mess with me!”
* In headlines, Hungarian often **drops articles** and auxiliary words, making them feel dense but efficient—very similar to English news style.
